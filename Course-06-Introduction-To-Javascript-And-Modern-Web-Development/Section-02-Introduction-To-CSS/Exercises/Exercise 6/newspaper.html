How Do Floats in HTML Work?

Imagine that you have a tiny bookshelf to hold your books. Since you are obviously a bright scholar, your books are quite thick so each row of your bookshelf can only hold 8 books at a time before you need to start adding books to the another row. We'll pretend that this bookshelf is an HTML page and by placing books on the shelf, we'll explore the concept of floating elements using CSS.

Starting at the topmost row, we place a book on the shelf. The book won't stand up properly in the middle of the shelf so we now have a choice - we can move the book to either the right or left side of the shelf. Similarly, with CSS, we can move elements to the right or left side of the row they reside in on an HTML page and this concept is known as floating.

Back to our bookshelf, let's say we moved the first book on the shelf to the left. If we add another book to the shelf and move it to the left, it will be placed in front of the one we already added. We'll also add two more books, this time moving both to the right side of the shelf. The second book that was moved to the right will be placed directly in front of the first. By this analogy, we can see that books can be added to the shelf and moved to the left or right until the row is full (8 books fill the row). After the row is full, new books can only be added to the next available row that has free space.

HTML is quite similar. The page has a fixed width and elements can be added to a row on the page and floated to the left or right until the row is full i.e. the combined width of all the elements placed in the row exceed the page width. Afterward, new elements will have to be added to new rows that have free space. Just like our example with the book shelf, elements on an HTML page are added from the top go down and elements that are floated to either side after other elements have already been floated are just placed in front of those before them.

While floating in HTML may be a difficult topic to grasp, I hope that this analogy gives some insight into how it works. There's a lot more to learn about floating elements however we'll save that for another time. Happy Coding!
